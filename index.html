about me

My current interests consist of human-centered design, public health, and linguistics. As of now, I am a research apprentice in the UC Berkeley Linguistics Department. Under the guidance of a PhD student, we are working on a project to create an online corpus for Unangam Tunuu (Aleut) in order to document and preserve the language of a historically oppressed indigenous people.

Some future pursuits include artificial intelligence, vision science, and natural language processing.

Feel free to check out my resume for my past experiences.

extra, extra

I spent much of my adolescent life immersed in dabbling in the arts---playing four instruments, learning Chinese calligraphy from my grandmother, sketching Disney princesses on scratch paper. Moved by my mother’s passion for cultural dance, I eventually settled on dancing and haven’t stopped since. This past year of college, I joined a competitive hip-hop team, Identity X, and captained during my second term. One thing I am grateful for in my life is the opportunity to experience so much art, and through it, love and inspiration.
